# BUYER-PROFILES

*The missing layer most sales libraries skip. You can master every framework in BOOKS, SPEAKERS, and STRATEGIES — and still under-perform if you sell the same way to a 28-year-old software developer in Santurce as you do to a 67-year-old construction-company *dueño* in Bayamón. They want different things. They speak different languages. They make decisions on different timelines using different criteria. This section maps the differences.*

## What this section covers

Seven complementary lenses for reading a buyer before you pitch them. Read all seven on any consequential deal:

1. **[Wealth-Class Segmentation](./01-Wealth-Class-Segmentation.md)** — The most important and most ignored lens. *Sell time to the rich. Sell aspiration to the middle class. Sell hope to the working class.* This file unpacks what each segment actually buys, what they fear, and what to never sell them.
2. **[Maslow & Six Human Needs](./02-Maslow-and-Human-Needs.md)** — What level of human need are you addressing? Robbins's Six Needs + Maslow's hierarchy = your motivation map.
3. **[DISC Personality Profiles](./03-DISC-Personality-Profiles.md)** — Driver / Influencer / Steady / Compliant. Read it from the buyer's first 90 seconds; calibrate the rest of the conversation.
4. **[Generational Profiles](./04-Generational-Profiles.md)** — Boomer (1946–64), Gen X (1965–80), Millennial (1981–96), Gen Z (1997–2012). Each generation buys differently — channel, pace, proof type, language.
5. **[B2B Decision Roles](./05-B2B-Decision-Roles.md)** — CEO, CFO, COO, end-user, champion, gatekeeper, blocker. Every B2B deal has all seven roles; ignore one and the deal stalls.
6. **[Puerto Rico Buyer Archetypes](./06-Puerto-Rico-Buyer-Archetypes.md)** — The local-market personas: the *dueño*, the professional, the *abuela*, the diaspora returnee, the Act 60 transplant, the *cangrejo*, the *resolvedor*.
7. **[Quick Reference Matrix](./07-Quick-Reference-Matrix.md)** — The cheat sheet. One page. Cross-tabbed: segment × what they buy × what to lead with × what to avoid.

## How to use this section

Before any sales conversation worth more than 30 minutes of your time, run a quick mental profile:

1. **Wealth class** — affluent, middle, working, subsistence?
2. **Need level** — are you selling safety, belonging, esteem, or self-actualization?
3. **DISC** — are they fast and decisive (D), warm and people-focused (I), steady and harmony-seeking (S), or analytical and risk-averse (C)?
4. **Generation** — does their decision style match their age cohort?
5. **B2B role** (if applicable) — who else is in the room you can't see?
6. **PR archetype** — which local persona does this buyer most resemble?

Thirty seconds of pre-meeting profiling outperforms thirty minutes of generic discovery.

## The ethical floor

These frameworks are powerful enough to be misused. The repository's bias is explicit: **influence techniques are for helping the buyer make a better decision, not for extracting value the buyer would regret.** The wealth-class file is particularly cautious about the working-class and subsistence segments — the same psychology that makes "sell hope" effective is what makes predatory lending, MLM exploitation, and lottery-style scams effective. *Boricuas* with sales skills carry responsibility for how they use them, especially on the island where reputations propagate fast.
