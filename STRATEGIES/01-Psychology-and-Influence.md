# 01 — Psychology and Influence

*The operating system underneath every sale. Master this layer and everything tactical compounds.*

## Why this playbook exists

Most "sales training" jumps straight to scripts, closes, and objection handling. That's like teaching someone to play chess by drilling openings without explaining why the center of the board matters. Influence works because of consistent, observable mechanisms in human psychology — not because of clever phrasing. This playbook condenses the canonical research and field-tested practice into a working operator's manual.

## The seven foundational levers

Synthesized from Cialdini's *Influence* (the academic foundation) and refined through Carnegie, Ziglar, Voss, Klaff, and Cabane:

### 1. Reciprocity
People feel obligated to return value received. Give first, give specifically, give without expectation — then ask. In sales practice: deliver insight, audit, sample, introduction, or analysis *before* the pitch. The pitch is permitted because reciprocity has been activated.

### 2. Commitment & Consistency
Once someone takes a small public step toward something, they unconsciously align larger behaviors with that step. Operationalize via micro-yeses, written declarations of interest, and stair-step pilots that lock in commitment before the big ask.

### 3. Social Proof
In uncertainty, people copy what similar others do. Sell *like* cases, not abstract benefits. "Three companies your size in Caguas adopted this last quarter" outperforms "this is the leading solution."

### 4. Authority
People defer to credible expertise. Earn it (credentials, track record, body of work) and signal it appropriately (case studies, specific outcomes, published material). Don't borrow authority you can't deliver.

### 5. Liking
We buy from people we like, and we like people who are: similar to us, warm toward us, and competent. Carnegie's entire system operationalizes this lever.

### 6. Scarcity
Loss aversion is roughly 2x stronger than gain attraction (Kahneman). Frame what the buyer **loses by not deciding**, not just what they gain by deciding. Honest scarcity (real deadline, real inventory) only — fake scarcity destroys trust.

### 7. Unity
Cialdini's seventh principle (added 2016). Shared identity — family, faith, region, profession, generation — short-circuits skepticism faster than any other lever. "Soy de Arecibo también" is a sales asset.

## The Robbins layer: state precedes tactics

None of the levers above work if the seller is in a low state. Tony Robbins' contribution is non-optional: **physiology + focus + language = state**. Run a 90-second pre-call routine (breath, posture, incantation, music) every time. State management is not motivation — it's plumbing.

## The Voss layer: tactical empathy

Never-Split-the-Difference principles overlay on the seven levers:
- **Mirroring** — repeat last 1–3 words; buyer expands.
- **Labeling** — "It sounds like timing is the concern" — names the emotion and disarms it.
- **Calibrated questions** — "How am I supposed to do that?" forces the buyer to solve your problem.
- **"That's right"** — the only verbal cue of actual alignment. "You're right" is dismissal.

## The Klaff layer: frame control

From *Pitch Anything*: every interaction has a frame, and frames collide. The dominant frame wins. Hold frame by: not seeking approval, withholding (don't dump everything), demonstrating prizing (you choose them, not the other way around), and resetting status when the buyer attempts to dominate.

## The Cabane layer: charisma is engineered

From *The Charisma Myth*: charisma = Presence + Power + Warmth. All three are trainable. Presence is the most important and the most neglected — the buyer must feel you are fully there with them, not running internal commentary or planning your next line.

## Puerto Rico application

- **Liking and Unity** are the dominant levers locally. Reciprocity is strong but slower-acting (longer trust cycle). Scarcity is the most easily abused — use sparingly and honestly.
- **Spanish-language psychology vocabulary** lands harder than English equivalents. *Confianza, respeto, palabra, compromiso, integridad* are not synonyms for trust/respect/word — they carry deeper cultural weight. Use them.
- **The first 90 seconds** of any PR sales call should be relational, not informational. *¿Cómo está la familia? ¿Y los muchachos?* before any business framing. Skipping this step costs more deals than any pricing objection.
- **Authority is regional.** A mainland MBA matters less than a documented track record with similar PR clients. Build local case studies and lead with them.
- **Tactical empathy in Spanish:** labels work beautifully (*"Parece que el momento es el reto"*), mirroring works (repeat the *muletilla* the buyer just used), and *"así mismo es"* is the local equivalent of "that's right."

## How to use this playbook

1. **Week 1–2:** master the seven Cialdini levers. Tag each in your last 20 deals and identify which lever was active (or absent).
2. **Week 3–4:** install Robbins pre-call state routine. Measure close-rate variance between high-state and low-state calls.
3. **Week 5–8:** drill Voss techniques (mirroring/labeling/calibrated Qs) on every call. Record yourself.
4. **Week 9–12:** add frame control (Klaff) for high-stakes deals. Practice on internal stakeholders first.
5. **Quarterly:** re-read Carnegie. The fundamentals don't expire.
