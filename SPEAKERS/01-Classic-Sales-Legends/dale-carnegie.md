# Dale Carnegie

**Lifespan:** 1888–1955  
**Category:** Classic Sales Legend — The Original American Influence Trainer  
**Status:** Founder of Dale Carnegie Training (still operating in over 80 countries); author of *How to Win Friends and Influence People*

---

## Core Philosophy

> "You can make more friends in two months by becoming interested in other people than you can in two years by trying to get other people interested in you."

Carnegie's premise was radical for the early 20th century: human relations is a *teachable craft*. Sincere interest in others, intelligent listening, and emotional empathy can be developed by anyone willing to practice — and they produce both better business outcomes and better lives.

## Signature Frameworks

- **Don't criticize, condemn, or complain.**
- **Give honest, sincere appreciation.**
- **Arouse in the other person an eager want.**
- **Become genuinely interested in other people.**
- **Remember that a person's name is the sweetest sound in any language.**
- **Be a good listener. Encourage others to talk about themselves.**
- **Talk in terms of the other person's interests.**

## Key Works

- *How to Win Friends and Influence People*
- *How to Stop Worrying and Start Living*
- *Public Speaking and Influencing Men in Business*

## Speaking & Training Style

Carnegie's training format — small groups, deliberate practice, peer feedback, repetition over weeks — became the template for nearly every modern professional-development program in existence. He taught millions through evening classes long before "sales training" was an industry.

## Why Puerto Rico Sellers Should Study Carnegie

Carnegie's empathy-first, criticism-free, sincere-interest framework is the *exact* cultural register that succeeds in Puerto Rico. The Dale Carnegie organization still operates active training programs in San Juan, and the alumni network reads like a who's-who of PR's senior business community.

## Legacy

The single most-quoted business author of all time. Every modern sales psychology book — from Cialdini to Voss to Carnegie's own franchise — stands on the foundation he laid in 1936.
