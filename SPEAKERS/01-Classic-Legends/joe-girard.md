# Joe Girard

**Era:** 1963–1978 (active selling), then speaking until 2019 · **Legacy:** Guinness World Record — World's Greatest Salesman · **Section:** Classic Legends

## Overview

Joe Girard sold 13,001 cars at a single Chevrolet dealership in Detroit between 1963 and 1977, all retail, one at a time — a record certified by Guinness and never broken. He started broke at age 35, kicked out of his apartment with a pregnant wife, and walked into a dealership begging for a chance. He left fifteen years later as the most productive single-unit retail seller in history. After retiring he toured the speaking circuit until his death in 2019.

## Core philosophy

**The Law of 250.** Every person knows roughly 250 other people. Treat one customer badly and you've insulted 250. Treat one customer right and 250 hear about it. Selling, for Girard, was a referral business pretending to be a transaction business. Every sale was the start of the next ten.

## Signature methods

- **The 250 Rule.** The single mental model that drove every decision.
- **The Bird-Dog network.** Girard paid $25 per referred customer. Built a self-replicating sales force of his own former customers, mechanics, barbers, and gas station attendants.
- **Mass mailing.** Sent 13,000+ personal greeting cards every month — birthdays, anniversaries, holidays — to stay top-of-mind.
- **"I like you."** That was the message on every card. Just three words. Hand-signed.
- **Relentless follow-up.** Customer bought a car? They got contact from Joe quarterly for the rest of their life.

## Puerto Rico application

The Law of 250 is **arguably stronger in Puerto Rico than in Detroit.** On a 3.2M-population island where everyone knows someone in three steps and reputation travels via WhatsApp in minutes, Girard's calculus understates the effect — call it the Law of 500 here. His monthly card discipline maps perfectly to a **monthly WhatsApp check-in** for modern PR sellers: birthday wishes, holiday greetings (*Día de Reyes, Acción de Gracias, Navidad*), a hurricane-season "¿están bien?" message after every storm. The bird-dog model (paying referral fees) is culturally smooth — *propinas por referido* is already informal practice in many PR industries; Girard just formalizes it. Train sellers: your existing 50 customers, treated like Girard treated his, will out-produce 500 cold calls.
