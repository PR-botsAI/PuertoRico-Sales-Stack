# Dale Carnegie

**Era:** 1912–1955 (active teaching) · **Legacy:** Father of modern interpersonal sales training · **Section:** Classic Legends

## Overview

Dale Carnegie didn't just write a book — he built the curriculum every sales trainer since has remixed. Starting in 1912 with a public-speaking course at a YMCA in New York, he assembled what became the Dale Carnegie Course: a structured, repeatable program for ordinary people to become persuasive, confident, and well-liked. *How to Win Friends and Influence People* (1936) crystallized the doctrine and has sold over 30 million copies. Carnegie's organization still operates today across 90+ countries.

## Core philosophy

*"You can make more friends in two months by becoming interested in other people than you can in two years by trying to get other people interested in you."* That sentence is the entire system. Selling is downstream of likability; likability is downstream of genuine interest; genuine interest is a practiced habit, not a trait.

## Signature methods

- **Don't criticize, condemn, or complain.** Carnegie's first principle. In sales, this kills the urge to argue with prospects.
- **Use the person's name.** A small mechanical habit with outsized warmth effect.
- **Talk in terms of the other person's interests.** The original "discovery question."
- **Make the other person feel important — sincerely.** The sincerity caveat is what separates Carnegie from manipulators.
- **Let the other person feel the idea is theirs.** Pre-Voss, pre-SPIN, this was already canon.

## Puerto Rico application

Carnegie's principles are **frictionless in Spanish.** The relational opener (*"¿Cómo está la familia?"*) is already PR default behavior — Carnegie just systematizes it. Use his name principle aggressively: in PR business culture, calling someone *don/doña* + first name lands with more weight than the English-language equivalent. His "let them feel important sincerely" rule is critical when calling on *dueños* of family businesses, who often haven't had a corporate seller treat them with proper deference in years. Avoid the rookie mistake of using Carnegie tactics performatively — *boricuas* detect *falsedad* in two sentences.
