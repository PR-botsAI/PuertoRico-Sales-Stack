# Robert Kiyosaki

**Born:** 1947  
**Category:** Business Icon / Speaker / Financial Educator  
**Status:** Author of *Rich Dad Poor Dad* — one of the best-selling personal finance books of all time

---

## Core Philosophy

> "The rich don't work for money. They make money work for them."

Kiyosaki's premise: the financial education taught in schools is engineered to produce employees, not wealth-builders. His central distinction — *assets put money in your pocket; liabilities take money out* — reframes the entire personal financial conversation around cash flow rather than appearance.

## Signature Frameworks

- **The Cashflow Quadrant** — Employee, Self-employed, Business owner, Investor. Most people live in the left side; wealth is built on the right.
- **Assets vs. liabilities** — The house you live in is a liability; the house you rent out is an asset.
- **Financial literacy as the missing curriculum** — Tax, accounting, market mechanics — deliberately under-taught.
- **Sales is the #1 skill of the entrepreneur** — Kiyosaki repeatedly emphasizes that selling is the highest-leverage skill for anyone wanting financial independence.

## Key Works

- *Rich Dad Poor Dad*
- *Cashflow Quadrant*
- *The Business of the 21st Century*
- *Rich Dad's Guide to Investing*

## Speaking Style

Direct, contrarian, occasionally provocative. Kiyosaki challenges audiences to question the financial defaults they inherited and is unafraid to disagree publicly with conventional financial advice.

## Why Puerto Rico Sellers and Entrepreneurs Should Study Kiyosaki

Kiyosaki's framework is *especially* relevant to PR sellers thinking about long-term wealth. The island offers distinctive tax incentives (Act 60 / formerly Acts 20 and 22), strong rental property markets, and growing entrepreneurial infrastructure. For high-earning commission sellers, Kiyosaki's asset-building doctrine is the bridge from "high income" to "compounding wealth."

## Legacy

Kiyosaki has minted more independently-minded investors and small-business owners than perhaps any other modern financial educator. His sales emphasis — *selling is the #1 entrepreneurial skill* — is the philosophical permission slip many introverted entrepreneurs need to develop the craft seriously.
