# 04 — Business Icons

These aren't sales trainers — they're operators. Each built a major business (or several) and along the way said things, wrote things, or made decisions that taught the rest of the world something durable about persuasion, deal-making, and customer creation. Sellers who study only sales trainers develop tactical skill but operational naïveté; pairing the tactical canon with this section is what produces a seller who can also *think like an owner*.

## Profiles in this section

- **[Mark Cuban](./mark-cuban.md)** — Bag-business hustler → Broadcast.com → Mavericks → Shark Tank investor. Cost-discipline and customer-obsession at scale.
- **[Richard Branson](./richard-branson.md)** — Virgin Group. Brand as the asset. Service experience as the differentiator.
- **[George Foreman](./george-foreman.md)** — Two-time heavyweight champion who endorsed a grill to a $200M+ payout. Authenticity in endorsement is the masterclass.
- **[Robert Kiyosaki](./robert-kiyosaki.md)** — *Rich Dad Poor Dad*. Financial literacy as the foundation under sales income.
- **[James Caan](./james-caan.md)** — UK *Dragon's Den* investor; recruitment and private equity. The hiring-and-deal-making lens.
- **[Daymond John](./daymond-john.md)** — FUBU founder → Shark Tank. The Power of Broke. Distribution and brand-building from zero capital.
- **[Ted Turner](./ted-turner.md)** — CNN founder. Counter-positioning, betting on category creation, billboard-to-empire scaling.

## How to use this section

These profiles are shorter on technique, heavier on context and *what they prove*. Read them as case studies in distribution, brand, and deal-making. Pair each with the relevant book or strategy file to operationalize.

## Puerto Rico relevance

The icons offer **different lessons depending on PR business archetype:**

- **Cuban + Branson** for the entrepreneur scaling a service business.
- **Foreman** for anyone leveraging personal brand into product (huge opportunity for PR influencers).
- **Kiyosaki** as foundation reading for any commission seller — sales income without asset-building is a high-paid treadmill.
- **Caan + John** for the founder learning to hire and partner well.
- **Turner** as the contrarian case study for category creation — directly relevant for PR-first businesses targeting underserved Spanish-speaking markets.
