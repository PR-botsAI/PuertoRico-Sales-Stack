# Mark Cuban

**Born:** 1958  
**Category:** Business Icon / Entrepreneur / Speaker  
**Status:** Self-made billionaire (sold Broadcast.com to Yahoo for $5.7B in 1999); owner of the Dallas Mavericks; *Shark Tank* investor; one of the most-watched modern business voices

---

## Core Philosophy

> "Every no gets me closer to a yes."

Cuban's premise: success is the function of *out-working* the competition combined with *out-thinking* them. He rejects luck-based narratives in favor of obsessive learning, fast iteration, and ruthless customer focus.

## Signature Themes

- **Sweat equity is the most valuable equity** — The willingness to learn, work, and adapt is the founder's strongest currency.
- **Sales cures all** — Every business problem is a sales problem in disguise.
- **Read what your competitors don't** — Cuban famously reads 3+ hours a day. The compounding advantage is enormous.
- **Customer obsession** — Win one customer's love and they'll bring you the next ten.
- **Speed is the founder's weapon** — Big companies can't move fast. Small companies can. Use it.

## Speaking Style

Blunt, fast, contrarian, and tactical. Cuban rejects motivational stagecraft — he speaks like an operator who is genuinely impatient to get back to work.

## Why Puerto Rico Entrepreneurs and Sellers Should Study Cuban

Cuban's voice is the modern Carnegie for digitally-native entrepreneurs. His insistence that *sales cures all* is the exact frame every PR business owner needs when revenue stalls. His emphasis on reading, learning, and out-working competitors is universally applicable — and the PR market, where many competitors *don't* read or invest in their craft, rewards Cuban's discipline disproportionately.

## Legacy

Cuban remains one of the most accessible self-made billionaires in business media. His ongoing public commentary, *Shark Tank* deals, and writing continue to shape how a generation of entrepreneurs thinks about sales, scale, and customer focus.
