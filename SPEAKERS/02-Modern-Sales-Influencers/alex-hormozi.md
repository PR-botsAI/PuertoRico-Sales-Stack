# Alex Hormozi

**Born:** 1988  
**Category:** Modern Sales/Marketing Strategist  
**Status:** Founder of Acquisition.com; author of *$100M Offers* and *$100M Leads*; one of the most-watched business educators on the internet

---

## Core Philosophy

> "You don't have a sales problem. You have an offer problem."

Hormozi's premise: the highest-leverage variable in any business is the *construction of the offer*. Engineer a grand-slam offer and ordinary sellers produce extraordinary results. He has built a multi-hundred-million-dollar fitness and licensing portfolio applying this principle across industries.

## Signature Frameworks

- **The Value Equation** — (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice).
- **Grand Slam Offers** — Bonus stacking + guarantees + urgency/scarcity + naming.
- **The Core Four lead sources** — Warm outreach, posting content, cold outreach, paid ads.
- **Rule of 100** — 100 outbound actions per day, across the Core Four, regardless of skill.
- **Affiliate → Agencies → Employees → Customers** — The sequence of lead-generating teams.

## Speaking Style

Monotone, dense, fast-paced, and analytically rigorous. Hormozi rejects motivational stagecraft in favor of dense framework delivery. His content is built to be re-watched, screenshotted, and operationalized.

## Why Puerto Rico Sellers Should Study Hormozi

Hormozi is the modern operating manual for PR entrepreneurs, agency owners, and senior sellers ready to break out of commodity competition. His offer-construction frameworks are particularly devastating in PR markets where local categories (gyms, clinics, contractors, digital services, restaurants) are dense with undifferentiated offers. The first operator in each PR category to apply Hormozi's frameworks captures the category.

## Legacy

Hormozi's contribution to modern sales is the formalization of *offer engineering* as a discipline distinct from sales execution and marketing creative. He proved that the same product, repackaged, can sell for 10x — and gave the industry a vocabulary for how.
