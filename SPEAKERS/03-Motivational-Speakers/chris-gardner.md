# Chris Gardner

**Born:** 1954  
**Category:** Motivational Speaker / Author / Entrepreneur  
**Status:** Subject of *The Pursuit of Happyness* (the 2006 film starring Will Smith); founder of Gardner Rich & Co.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "You got a dream, you gotta protect it. People can't do something themselves, they wanna tell you you can't do it. You want something, go get it. Period."

Gardner's career arc — from homeless single father sleeping in subway bathrooms to multi-million-dollar stockbroker — is the living proof of his message: circumstance is not destiny. Persistence, dignity, and the refusal to abandon a dream are the active ingredients in transformation.

## Signature Themes

- **Protect your dream** — Especially from people who couldn't achieve theirs.
- **The world is full of dream-stealers** — Choose your inner circle accordingly.
- **Cavalry isn't coming** — Self-rescue is the only reliable rescue.
- **Dignity through any circumstance** — Gardner went to job interviews in his only suit after sleeping in a public bathroom the night before.
- **Fatherhood as fuel** — The single most powerful motivator in his story is responsibility for his son.

## Speaking Style

Measured, dignified, story-driven, and deeply personal. Gardner doesn't shout — he testifies. His delivery carries the weight of lived experience, and his keynotes are some of the most emotionally credible in the modern speaking circuit.

## Why Puerto Rico Sellers Should Study Gardner

For sellers facing real personal hardship while trying to build a sales career on the island — post-hurricane recovery, economic stress, family responsibility — Gardner's story is uniquely actionable. He is not a privileged motivator; he is a man who walked through the fire and arrived. His message gives permission to keep showing up.

## Legacy

Gardner's story has become a cultural shorthand for *earned* upward mobility through persistence. His foundation, books, and speaking career continue to extend that message globally.
