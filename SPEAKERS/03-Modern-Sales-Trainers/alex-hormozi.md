# Alex Hormozi

**Era:** 2013–present · **Legacy:** The offer-engineer and acquisition strategist of the 2020s · **Section:** Modern Sales Trainers

## Overview

Alexander Hormozi built and exited a gym-licensing company (Gym Launch), a supplement brand (Prestige Labs), and a software company (ALAN), and now operates Acquisition.com — a portfolio holding company investing in 8- and 9-figure businesses. His public-facing work over the last four years (free books, daily long-form content, the Game podcast) has reset how a generation of founders, marketers, and sellers think about offer construction and customer acquisition. Note that his published methods are recent; the durability over decades is still being proven — but the current influence on practice is undeniable.

## Core philosophy

**"Make people an offer so good they'd feel stupid saying no."** Hormozi argues most pricing/conversion problems are actually offer problems. A weak offer requires heroic selling; a strong offer largely sells itself. Engineer the offer first; obsess over the sales script second.

## Signature methods

- **The Value Equation:** Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). Increase the numerator, decrease the denominator.
- **The Grand Slam Offer:** combines the Value Equation with bonuses, scarcity, urgency, guarantees, and naming.
- **Core Four channels** of lead generation: warm outreach, content for warm audiences, cold outreach, paid ads.
- **Lead magnet types:** give something away that solves a known problem to capture a specific demographic.
- **CAC < LTV/3** as the operational test for sustainable acquisition.
- **Volume negates luck.** The variance disappears when you do enough reps.

## Puerto Rico application

Hormozi's Value Equation is **the single highest-leverage framework** for PR entrepreneurs operating in a price-sensitive market. The local instinct under margin pressure is to drop price; the Hormozi response is to **stack perceived value** (dream outcome clarity, social proof of likelihood, faster delivery, less customer effort) until the same price feels cheap. For PR coaches, agencies, gyms, professional services, and any local SaaS or info-product play, this reframing is transformational. His Core Four channels also map well: warm outreach (referrals from family/professional networks — huge in PR), warm content (Spanish-language Instagram/TikTok — enormous untapped opportunity), cold outreach (WhatsApp + email — effective if disciplined), paid ads (cheaper CPMs in Spanish than English in most categories). Caveat: Hormozi's guarantees, scarcity, and urgency mechanics need cultural calibration — aggressive scarcity copy reads as *trampa* to skeptical PR buyers; soften the framing while keeping the structure.
