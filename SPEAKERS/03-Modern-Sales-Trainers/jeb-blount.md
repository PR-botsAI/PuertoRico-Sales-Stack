# Jeb Blount

**Era:** 2007–present · **Legacy:** The dean of modern prospecting · **Section:** Modern Sales Trainers

## Overview

Jeb Blount founded Sales Gravy in 2007 and has spent the last two decades systematizing the unglamorous discipline most sellers avoid: prospecting. Where Hormozi engineers offers and Cardone fuels activity, Blount engineers **the daily mechanics** — phone, email, video, social, and in-person sequences — that actually fill pipelines. He's a top global sales speaker, advises Fortune 500 clients, and has authored over a dozen books with consistent practical depth.

## Core philosophy

**You must prospect every day.** Pipeline anxiety, end-of-quarter scrambles, and roller-coaster income are all symptoms of one root cause: inconsistent prospecting. The cure isn't tools or scripts — it's a non-negotiable daily prospecting block, treated like surgery: scheduled, blocked, executed.

## Signature methods

- **The 30-Day Rule.** Activity this 30 days produces results in the *following* 90 days. Stop prospecting today, feel the empty pipe in three months.
- **The Universal Law of Need.** The needier the seller appears, the lower the close rate. Full pipeline = confidence = better close rates.
- **Five Cs of social selling:** Connect, Curate, Communicate, Create, Convert.
- **Telephone, email, social, in-person, referral, networking, inbound.** All seven channels run in parallel — not as alternatives.
- **Sales EQ.** Emotional discipline is the differentiator at the elite level. Manage your state, manage the buyer's state.
- **Objections framework.** Reflexive responses memorized in advance; specifically taught for RBOs (Reflex), micro-commitments, and buying-process objections.

## Puerto Rico application

Blount's omnichannel methodology is **the closest fit to PR sales reality** of any modern trainer. The 7-channel framework maps directly onto how PR buyers actually communicate: phone calls still convert (older buyers prefer voice), email works for institutional and corporate prospects, **WhatsApp is the dominant channel for SMB/family-business outreach**, in-person is still highly valued for high-ticket and trust-heavy sales, referrals propagate fast on a small island, networking events (chamber, industry associations) carry weight, and inbound is wide-open opportunity for any seller producing Spanish-language content. Adopt his 30-Day Rule literally: build a daily prospecting block, protect it as you would a doctor's appointment, and measure leading indicators (dials, touches, conversations) not lagging (closed deals) weekly. His objection-handling framework (memorized reflexive responses) is particularly important for bilingual sellers who otherwise hesitate switching between Spanish and English under pressure.
