# Go-Givers Sell More
**Authors:** Bob Burg & John David Mann  
**First published:** 2010  
**Section:** Billionaire & Top-Performer Recommendations

## Overview
The operational sequel to *The Go-Giver*. Where the original is a parable about a philosophy, this is the working seller's manual that translates the Five Laws into specific behaviors on calls, in meetings, and across long-cycle relationships.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Shift the focus to the other person
The seller's mind, on every call, should be **on the prospect's interests — not on the seller's commission.** This is internally enforced. The reorientation is visible to the prospect within seconds and reshapes the entire dynamic.

### 2. The "why" of buying
People don't buy *what* you do; they buy why **they** would do it. The seller's job is to understand the prospect's reason and reflect it back.

### 3. Stretching the value
For any product, the value the prospect ultimately receives is much greater than the immediate transaction. The seller's job is to articulate the **downstream value** — second-order effects, time saved, problems prevented — not just the immediate deliverable.

### 4. The 30-second "how I help" statement
Burg's framework for elevator-pitch positioning: clear, specific, focused on the type of person you serve and the type of outcome you produce. Not "I sell insurance" but "I help young families make sure they're financially protected if the worst happens — so they can stop worrying about it and get on with their lives."

### 5. Don't sell — educate, encourage, reassure
Three modes of go-giver conversation:
- **Educate** — help the prospect see the situation more clearly.
- **Encourage** — affirm their capacity to make a good decision.
- **Reassure** — reduce friction by demonstrating safety in the path forward.

## Why This Still Works in 2026
In a market saturated with manipulative outreach, the go-giver stance reads as credible specifically because so few sellers operate this way. Differentiation through ethics is structurally viable.

## Quotables
- "People want to buy. They just don't want to be sold."
- "Position yourself as the trusted advisor first — the transaction comes second, naturally."

## Puerto Rico Application
- The 30-second statement translates cleanly into Spanish and resonates strongly with PR's preference for relational, story-anchored introductions.
- **Educate-encourage-reassure** is structurally consistent with how PR's most respected sellers (life insurance brokers, financial advisors, family-business consultants) already operate.

## How to Use It
- Pair with *The Go-Giver* — read the parable first, then this for operational mechanics.
- Write your own 30-second "how I help" statement. Test it in 10 networking conversations. Refine.
