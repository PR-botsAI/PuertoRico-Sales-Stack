# Fanatical Prospecting
**Author:** Jeb Blount  
**First published:** 2015  
**Section:** Modern Sales Strategy

## Overview
The modern bible of prospecting discipline. Blount's central argument: **pipeline collapse is always preceded by prospecting collapse**, usually two or three months earlier. Sellers who maintain fanatical, daily, multi-channel prospecting discipline never have pipeline crises. Those who don't, do.

The book is the operational complement to Bettger's foundational work — same gospel of activity, modernized for omnichannel reality.

## Author Context
Jeb Blount runs Sales Gravy, one of the largest sales-training organizations globally. He works directly with enterprise sellers and has a hard-won perspective on what actually moves pipeline numbers.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The 30-Day Rule
The activity you do in the next 30 days determines your pipeline 60–90 days from now. Most sellers' "slow quarter" was set in motion before they realized it. Conversely, today's prospecting block is paying for a deal that closes nine weeks from now.

### 2. The Universal Law of Need
"The more you need a sale, the less likely you are to get it." Need radiates from the seller and triggers buyer defense. The cure is full pipeline; the cure for full pipeline is fanatical prospecting.

### 3. The 5 Cs of Social Selling
Connect → Curate (post relevant content) → Comment (on prospects' content) → Converse (move to DM) → Convert (to call / meeting). Each step in order; skipping creates rejection.

### 4. The phone is not dead
Blount makes the empirical case: phone conversion rates per attempt are still higher than every other channel for most B2B segments. The phone is underused, not obsolete.

### 5. Time blocking
Prospecting is done in dedicated, defended, recurring blocks — ideally morning, ideally daily, no exceptions. Open calendars get filled with everything except prospecting.

### 6. The objection-turnaround framework
For cold-call objections ("send me an email" / "not interested" / "call me next quarter"):
- **Acknowledge** the objection without arguing.
- **Bridge** to a value statement that reframes the conversation.
- **Ask** for a small commitment (15-minute call) instead of the big one.

### 7. Omnichannel prospecting
No single channel is enough. The modern prospecting sequence interlocks phone, email, LinkedIn, video, and referrals across 8–12 touches per prospect over 14–21 days.

## Why This Still Works in 2026
If anything, Blount's discipline has gotten more important. With AI-generated outreach saturating inboxes, **disciplined human-quality prospecting at consistent volume** is one of the highest-leverage things a seller can do.

## Puerto Rico Application
- **PR sellers often under-prospect** because the relational culture rewards in-person and referral work — which is great when it's there, fragile when it isn't. Blount's discipline is the insurance policy.
- **Phone is still highly effective in PR** for small-business and dealership verticals; pickup rates are higher than mainland in many segments.
- **WhatsApp Business** is a critical channel in PR not covered explicitly in the book — add it as a 6th channel in the omnichannel mix.

## How to Use It
- Block 90 minutes of prospecting daily before anything else. Treat it like surgery: scheduled, defended, no exceptions.
- Track your 30-day prospecting numbers against your 60–90-day pipeline. The correlation will appear clearly within one cycle.
- Build a 12-touch omnichannel sequence for each ICP segment.
