# $100M Offers
**Author:** Alex Hormozi  
**Full title:** *\$100M Offers: How To Make Offers So Good People Feel Stupid Saying No*  
**First published:** 2021  
**Section:** Modern Sales Strategy

## Overview
The defining offer-construction text of the 2020s. Hormozi's central insight: the offer itself — not the sales script, not the funnel, not the ad — is the single biggest leverage point in any business. Most companies have a mediocre offer wrapped in heroic marketing; flipping the ratio (Grand Slam offer with adequate marketing) transforms unit economics.

Hormozi built and sold a portfolio of companies producing over \$100M in annual revenue using these exact frameworks. The book reveals the playbook explicitly.

## Author Context
Alex Hormozi started in commission gym sales, scaled Gym Launch and ALAN as agencies, and then built Acquisition.com as a holding company for portfolio companies he scales using the offer mechanics in this book. He gives the book away free because the constraint isn't access; it's execution.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The Value Equation
Hormozi's central formula. Perceived value =

```
   (Dream Outcome) × (Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
  ----------------------------------------------------------
     (Time Delay) × (Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To increase perceived value, increase the numerator (better outcome, more certainty) or decrease the denominator (faster, easier). Most sellers only push on Dream Outcome; the highest leverage is usually in the denominator.

### 2. The Grand Slam Offer
An offer so disproportionately good that it is genuinely irrational to refuse it. Components:
- **A specific, valuable dream outcome** — the buyer's actual desired end state.
- **Stacked deliverables** that each solve a sub-problem on the path.
- **Risk reversal** — stronger than "money-back guarantee"; often performance-based or better-than-money-back.
- **Urgency and scarcity** — real, not manufactured.
- **Bonuses** that solve adjacent problems the buyer hasn't thought of yet.

### 3. The pricing principle
"Charge what it's worth, then deliver more than that." Underpricing is not generosity — it's a guarantee of poor delivery (you can't afford to over-serve) and weak perceived value (cheap reads as low-confidence).

### 4. Problem inversion
List every problem the buyer faces, then list the solution to each, then turn each solution into a stacked deliverable. The offer assembles itself from problem analysis.

### 5. Risk reversal hierarchy
- Conditional money-back → unconditional money-back → better-than-money-back (e.g., 2x refund) → performance-based (you don't pay unless you get the result). The further down you can credibly go, the more friction collapses.

## Why This Still Works in 2026
The attention economy has only intensified. Mediocre offers cannot survive the cost-of-acquisition math in 2026; only Grand Slam offers have the margin to absorb modern CAC and still grow.

## Puerto Rico Application
- **Hormozi's framework collapses PR's price-sensitivity objection** when applied properly. PR buyers don't refuse premium offers; they refuse undifferentiated offers at premium prices. The Grand Slam fixes both.
- **Risk reversal especially powerful in PR**, where trust-cycles are long. A genuine performance guarantee can be a category-defining advantage.
- **Stacked deliverables** map to PR's family-business preference for total-package solutions over component pricing.

## How to Use It
- Solo operators / founders: rebuild your highest-volume offer using the Value Equation. Quantify each numerator/denominator term.
- Sales teams: a Grand Slam offer makes selling 5–10x easier; the leverage is at the offer-design layer, not the rep layer.
- Marketing teams: every ad creative is downstream of the offer; fix offer first.
