# $100M Leads
**Author:** Alex Hormozi  
**Full title:** *\$100M Leads: How to Get Strangers To Want To Buy Your Stuff*  
**First published:** 2023  
**Section:** Modern Sales Strategy

## Overview
The companion to *\$100M Offers*. Where Offers solves "why would they buy," Leads solves "how do they find out about it." Hormozi's framework reduces all customer acquisition to four channels and provides scaling playbooks for each.

## Author Context
Hormozi positions Leads as the harder of the two problems. A great offer with no leads dies quietly; modest offer with brilliant lead-flow can survive long enough to fix the offer. He argues most operators massively underinvest in the lead-generation craft.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The four core lead-gen channels
- **Warm outreach** — contacting people who know you.
- **Posted content** — attention-grabbing media to people who don't.
- **Cold outreach** — contacting people who don't know you, one-to-one.
- **Paid ads** — buying attention at scale.

Each has different unit economics, ramp time, and skill requirements. Hormozi's advice: master one before adding the next.

### 2. The Lead Magnet
A standalone gift that solves a narrow, urgent problem in advance of the main offer. Strong lead magnets:
- Solve a problem **completely** within their scope.
- Create a clear next-problem the main offer addresses.
- Are tangible and rapidly consumed.
- Convert because the recipient experiences a small win.

### 3. The Core Four sequencing
Hormozi maps a maturity arc:
- Phase 1: Warm outreach + posted content (zero or low spend; high effort).
- Phase 2: Add cold outreach (operationalized prospecting).
- Phase 3: Add paid ads (scale).
- Phase 4: Hire and systematize each channel.

### 4. The "more better new" framework
When lead-flow underperforms, ask in order:
- Do **more** of what's currently working.
- Do **better** versions of what's currently working.
- Add **new** channels.

Most operators jump to "new" when "more" or "better" would solve the problem cheaper.

### 5. Affiliates / partners / employees as multipliers
Once the core engine works, multiply through people whose interests are aligned with your lead-flow:
- **Affiliates** earn per conversion.
- **Agency partners** package your offer.
- **Employees** as dedicated channel owners.

## Why This Still Works in 2026
Lead-gen channel volatility has increased — platforms shift algorithms, ad costs spike, organic reach decays. Operators with **mastery across all four channels** are resilient; single-channel operators are increasingly fragile.

## Puerto Rico Application
- **PR's relational economy makes warm outreach exceptionally high-leverage** — most local sellers underuse it because it feels too informal. Hormozi's framework re-legitimizes it as a strategic channel.
- **Spanish-language content posting** is wide-open in most PR verticals. Operators willing to publish consistently in Spanish for 12 months will dominate organic reach.
- **Local-market lead magnets** ("Guía para el dueño de \[business type\] en Puerto Rico") convert dramatically better than generic mainland-translated assets.

## How to Use It
- Map your current acquisition mix across the four channels. Identify which is strongest and double down before adding a second.
- Build one lead magnet per ICP segment, each designed around a single, urgent, narrowly-solvable problem.
- Establish weekly lead-flow metrics so the "more / better / new" diagnostic can run on real data.
