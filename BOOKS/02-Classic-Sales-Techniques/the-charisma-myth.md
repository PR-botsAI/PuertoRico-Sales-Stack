# The Charisma Myth

**Author:** Olivia Fox Cabane  
**First Published:** 2012  
**Era:** Modern — Behavioral Charisma Science  
**Status:** The book that proved charisma is a *learnable* skill set, not innate

---

## Core Thesis

Charisma is not magic. It is the *predictable* product of three components — **Presence, Power, and Warmth** — each of which can be deliberately trained. The author taught these skills at Harvard, MIT, and the United Nations to executives previously labeled "low-charisma."

## The Three Components

1. **Presence** — Full attention on the other person, free of distraction and inner chatter.
2. **Power** — Perceived ability to affect outcomes. Posture, voice, certainty.
3. **Warmth** — Genuine goodwill toward the other person.

## Key Tactics

- **Visualization:** Recall a moment of warmth before entering the meeting — the body language follows automatically.
- **The discomfort fix:** Before high-stakes calls, reframe physical anxiety as excitement. Same chemistry, different label.
- **Body language calibration:** Stand fully grounded. Slow speech. Pauses are dominant, not hesitant.
- **Recovery from awkwardness:** Don't ruminate. Reset internally; the other person will reset with you.

## Application to Modern Sales

- **Pre-call charisma protocol:** 90 seconds of presence training before high-stakes meetings.
- **Recording audits:** Reps watch themselves and catch low-power tells — fidgeting, vocal upticks, filler words.
- **Warmth as a closer's weapon:** In a tied-feature comparison, the warmer rep wins.

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

The blend of warmth + power is culturally optimal for PR business interactions. Cabane's framework gives reps an explicit method to produce both — something the local style produces naturally in extroverts but leaves introverts under-equipped. Excellent for technical-product reps (pharma, engineering, IT) who underperform on relational dimensions.

## Key Takeaways

- Charisma is trained, not born.
- Presence is the rarest commodity in modern selling.
- Warmth without power is sycophancy. Power without warmth is intimidation. Both together = charisma.
