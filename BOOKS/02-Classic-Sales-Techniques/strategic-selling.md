# The New Strategic Selling

**Authors:** Robert Miller & Stephen Heiman (with Tad Tuleja)  
**First Published:** 1985 (revised editions through 2005)  
**Era:** Foundational — First Formal Enterprise Sales Methodology  
**Status:** The original framework underlying nearly every modern enterprise sales process

---

## Core Thesis

Complex sales are *won by managing the buying committee*, not by impressing a single buyer. Every enterprise deal has multiple stakeholders — Economic Buyer, User Buyer, Technical Buyer, and Coach — each with different concerns. Map them, address them differently, and the deal closes.

## Key Concepts

- **The four buying influences:** Economic Buyer (gives final approval), User Buyer (uses the product), Technical Buyer (gatekeeper, says no), Coach (your internal ally).
- **Win-results vs. win-win:** Every stakeholder needs a personal win and a corporate result. Both must be addressed.
- **Red flags:** Missing roles, no coach, single contact — each is a structural risk to the deal.
- **The Blue Sheet:** A diagnostic document for every major opportunity.

## Application to Modern Sales

- **Account mapping:** Visual org charts annotated with role, influence, and stance toward your solution.
- **Coach development:** Your champion must be coached on internal selling — they need ammunition to advocate for you when you're not in the room.
- **Multi-threading:** Single-threaded deals (one contact only) lose 60% more often than multi-threaded deals.

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

Local enterprise buying — government, large corporates, university systems, healthcare networks — follows Miller/Heiman patterns exactly. The methodology helps reps navigate PR's particularly intricate stakeholder dynamics, where family relationships, political affiliations, and historical loyalties overlay formal org charts.

## Key Takeaways

- No single decision-maker exists in complex sales.
- Map the buying committee or lose the deal.
- Your coach is your most important asset — develop them deliberately.
