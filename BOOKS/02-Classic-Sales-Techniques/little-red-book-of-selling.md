# The Little Red Book of Selling
**Author:** Jeffrey Gitomer  
**First published:** 2004  
**Section:** Classic Sales Techniques

## Overview
Gitomer's manifesto on "12.5 principles of sales greatness." Short, punchy, and built for re-reading. The book's tone is closer to a daily devotional than a textbook — but its principles are dense, lived-in, and consistently quoted by working sellers.

If the foundational books are the curriculum, this is the pocket reference you keep on the desk.

## Key Concepts

### 1. People don't like to be sold — but they love to buy
The seller's entire job is to create conditions in which the prospect feels they are doing the buying. Pressure inverts the dynamic; curiosity preserves it.

### 2. Value first, value always
Gitomer's central call: stop selling and start serving. Provide value so disproportionate to the ask that the relationship is permanently asymmetric in the prospect's favor. The prospect closes themselves.

### 3. Become known as an expert
The modern seller is a publishing operation. Write, post, teach — your name should be recognized before the first cold call ever lands. This was prescient in 2004 and is now table stakes.

### 4. Get personal
Referrals beat cold calls 10:1; warm intros beat referrals 5:1. The personal network is the seller's most undervalued asset.

### 5. The 12.5 principles (selected highlights)
- Kick your own ass.
- Prepare to win, or lose to someone who is.
- Personal branding is sales.
- It's all about value, it's all about relationship, it's not about price.
- Be your own brand of remarkable.

## Why This Still Works in 2026
Gitomer's content-marketing prescription pre-dated LinkedIn-as-sales-channel by a decade. In 2026 the principles are not theory — they're the operating model of every top-decile B2B AE.
