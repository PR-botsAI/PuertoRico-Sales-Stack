# Integrity Selling for the 21st Century

**Author:** Ron Willingham  
**First Published:** 2003  
**Era:** Modern — Values-Based Selling  
**Status:** The corporate training curriculum used by Fortune 500 sales orgs

---

## Core Thesis

The salesperson's *belief system* about money, success, and worthiness sets the ceiling on their performance more than any tactical skill. Integrity selling is the alignment of values, behaviors, and intent — producing both higher income and a salesperson who can sleep at night.

## Key Concepts

- **The AID,Inc. process:** Approach, Interview, Demonstrate, Validate, Negotiate, Close. A repeatable consultative cycle.
- **Identity-level work:** "I am a person who serves others through skilled selling." Identity drives behavior more than tactics.
- **The values audit:** Misalignment between personal values and sales behaviors causes burnout, regardless of income.
- **Customer-centered emotional commitment:** Care deeply, push gently.

## Application to Modern Sales

- **Identity-first coaching:** Recruits who see themselves as professionals from day one outperform those who don't.
- **Values-aligned product portfolios:** Selling something you genuinely believe in is a multiplier on close rates.
- **Long-term retention:** Integrity reps stay 3-4x longer than tactical-only reps.

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

PR's tight community means a single integrity failure follows a salesperson for years. Willingham's framework is structural protection — it makes high performance *and* high reputation simultaneously achievable.

## Key Takeaways

- The amateur sells products. The professional sells trust.
- Values misalignment is the silent killer of sales careers.
- The customer always knows.
