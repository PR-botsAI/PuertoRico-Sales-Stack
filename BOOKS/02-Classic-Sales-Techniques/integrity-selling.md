# Integrity Selling for the 21st Century
**Author:** Ron Willingham  
**First published:** 1987 (modern edition 2003)  
**Section:** Classic Sales Techniques

## Overview
Willingham's central claim: traditional aggressive selling damages both buyer and seller. He proposes an alternative — a values-anchored, needs-focused model where the seller's role is genuinely diagnostic and the deal closes only if it serves the buyer.

Integrity Selling sits in the long lineage from Bettger (service) through Ziglar (help people get what they want) to Burg (giving). It's the ethical spine of the classic toolkit.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The AID,Inc. process
Willingham's six-step structure:
- **A** — Approach (relational opening, not pitch)
- **I** — Interview (deep needs discovery)
- **D** — Demonstrate (only after Interview, only what's relevant)
- **V** — Validate (proof, testimonials, references)
- **N** — Negotiate (objections worked through with empathy)
- **C** — Close (a logical next step once value is mutual)

### 2. Behavior follows beliefs
A seller cannot consistently sell ethically while privately holding manipulative beliefs about prospects. Willingham insists internal alignment is the foundation of external consistency.

### 3. The trust equation
Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation. The lower the seller's apparent self-orientation, the higher trust climbs. The math punishes commission-obvious behavior.

## Why This Still Works in 2026
B2B buyers increasingly run procurement processes designed specifically to detect manipulation. The integrity model isn't morally preferable — it's mathematically preferable in environments where trust signals are scored.

## Quotables
- "Most people sell from the wrong end — starting with their need to sell, not the buyer's need to solve."
- "Your highest commitment isn't to close — it's to discover."

## Puerto Rico Application
- **Trust-cycle length in PR rewards integrity selling.** PR's tight professional networks mean reputation propagates faster than mainland markets — a single burned customer costs disproportionately more.
- **The values frame translates naturally** to Spanish-language selling, where consultative and dignity-respecting language is the cultural default.

## How to Use It
- Onboarding: use AID,Inc. as the structural spine before introducing harder closing tactics.
- Self-audit: rate your last 10 deals on the trust equation — where is your self-orientation showing through?
