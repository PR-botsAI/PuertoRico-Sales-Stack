# Go-Givers Sell More

**Authors:** Bob Burg & John David Mann  
**First Published:** 2010 (companion to *The Go-Giver*, 2007)  
**Era:** Modern — Value-First Philosophy  
**Status:** The philosophical antidote to manipulative selling

---

## Core Thesis

The most effective salespeople focus relentlessly on *giving value* before *capturing value*. The Five Laws of Stratospheric Success aren't soft — they are the most reliable engine of compounding referrals, retention, and lifetime value in modern selling.

## The Five Laws

1. **The Law of Value** — Your true worth is determined by how much more you give in value than you take in payment.
2. **The Law of Compensation** — Your income is determined by how many people you serve and how well.
3. **The Law of Influence** — Your influence is determined by how abundantly you place others' interests first.
4. **The Law of Authenticity** — The most valuable thing you offer is yourself.
5. **The Law of Receptivity** — The key to effective giving is staying open to receiving.

## Application to Modern Sales

- **Value-first prospecting:** Send the prospect something useful (not branded) before asking for anything.
- **Referral architecture:** A go-giver builds a network of advocates, not a list of leads.
- **Long-game accounts:** The Five Laws compound. Year-one revenue may underperform; year-three revenue dominates.

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

The Go-Giver philosophy is essentially a formalization of how relationship-based business has always worked in Puerto Rico. *Hacer favores*, *ayudar sin esperar*, and family-style hospitality are cultural defaults — Burg and Mann simply systematize what top PR sellers do instinctively.

## Key Takeaways

- Give first. Track nothing. The math takes care of itself.
- Authenticity is the highest-leverage sales asset.
- Networks compound. Lists decay.
