# Fanatical Prospecting

**Author:** Jeb Blount  
**First Published:** 2015  
**Era:** Modern — Pipeline Discipline Doctrine  
**Status:** The modern definitive text on consistent pipeline building

---

## Core Thesis

The number one reason salespeople fail is an **empty pipeline**, and the number one reason for empty pipelines is reluctance to prospect. Blount's argument: prospecting is the highest-paid activity in sales, period — and the discipline of doing it daily, regardless of mood or quota status, is the single most reliable predictor of long-term success.

## Key Concepts

- **The 30-Day Rule:** What you do today shows up 30, 60, 90 days from now. Skip prospecting for three weeks and you'll feel it three months later.
- **The Law of Replacement:** You must constantly replace deals that close (or die) in your pipeline.
- **Five Cs of social selling:** Connect, Curate, Create, Curiosity, Consistency.
- **Time blocking is non-negotiable:** Prospecting calendar blocks are sacred. Nothing reschedules them.
- **Multi-channel attack:** Phone + email + LinkedIn + video + referral. Channels compound when combined.

## Application to Modern Sales

- **Daily prospecting block:** Top reps schedule 60-90 minutes of pure outbound every morning before email opens.
- **Pipeline ratio math:** If you close 20% of qualified opps, you need 5x your quota in pipeline at all times.
- **Channel orchestration:** Phone call + LinkedIn touch + personalized email within 48 hours — the modern "Triple Threat."

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

PR sales careers often die in the second year, when the personal network is exhausted and *referido*-driven momentum stalls. Blount's discipline is the bridge from network-fed selling to systematic pipeline building. Essential reading for any rep planning a 10+ year career on the island.

## Key Takeaways

- Prospect daily or perish quarterly.
- The discipline is the differentiator. Tactics are commodity.
- Channels stacked beat channels picked.
