# The Little Red Book of Selling

**Author:** Jeffrey Gitomer  
**First Published:** 2004  
**Era:** Modern — Punchy, Practical, Field-Ready  
**Status:** The book sales managers hand to new reps on day one

---

## Core Thesis

People don't like to be sold, but they love to buy. The salesperson's job is to make buying easy, friendly, and obvious — not to push, manipulate, or close. The book is 12.5 principles delivered in punchy, irreverent prose designed for reps who don't read books.

## The 12.5 Principles (highlights)

- **Kick your own ass.** Personal responsibility is the foundation. No external excuse holds up.
- **Prepare to win, or lose to someone who is.** Pre-call preparation is the entire game.
- **Personal branding is sales.** Your reputation precedes every call.
- **It's all about value, it's all about relationship, it's not all about price.**
- **Get networked or get not-worked.** Most deals come through warm referrals.
- **If you can make them laugh, you can make them buy.**
- **Use creativity to differentiate and dominate.**

## Application to Modern Sales

- **Personal brand on LinkedIn:** Gitomer was 15 years early on this. Every modern top closer maintains an audience.
- **Pre-call research as discipline:** 10 minutes of prep before every meaningful call.
- **Humor as differentiator:** Most reps are forgettable. Funny reps get callbacks.

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

Gitomer's emphasis on networking and personal branding is tailor-made for Puerto Rico's tightly-connected business community. On the island, your reputation walks into the room ten minutes before you do.

## Key Takeaways

- Personal responsibility is the first commission.
- Networking is not optional.
- Funny outperforms formal in 90% of buyer interactions.
