# Predictable Revenue

**Authors:** Aaron Ross & Marylou Tyler  
**First Published:** 2011  
**Era:** Modern — The SaaS Outbound Bible  
**Status:** Built the playbook that took Salesforce from $5M to $100M ARR

---

## Core Thesis

Founder-led selling does not scale. To build predictable, repeatable revenue, you must separate the sales function into specialized roles: **SDRs** (outbound prospectors), **AEs** (closers), and **CSMs** (renewers/expanders). This division of labor is the engineering of a sales machine.

## Key Concepts

- **Cold Calling 2.0:** Replace mass cold calling with researched, multi-touch outbound — referral-style emails to senior contacts asking for the right person.
- **Specialization:** A single rep cannot prospect, close, and manage accounts well. The roles require different skills, personalities, and incentives.
- **The seed/net/spear framework:** Seeds (customer success-driven referrals), Nets (marketing-generated inbound), Spears (outbound prospecting). A healthy pipeline uses all three.
- **Activity-based pipeline math:** Predictable revenue is the math of activity → meetings → opportunities → closed deals. Each ratio is engineered.

## Application to Modern Sales

- **SDR teams:** Every modern B2B org with a list price over $10K/year now runs an SDR motion. Ross designed it.
- **Email at scale:** Short, referral-style cold emails outperform feature-pitch emails by an order of magnitude.
- **Forecasting from ratios:** Once you know your funnel ratios, you can forecast quarterly revenue from this month's activity.

## Puerto Rico Market Relevance

PR's emerging tech sector, BPO/contact center industry, and B2B services firms are perfect candidates for Predictable Revenue's machinery. The island has a deep pool of bilingual talent that can serve both PR and mainland US markets as SDRs at competitive cost — a structural advantage Ross's model can scale dramatically.

## Key Takeaways

- Hope is not a forecast.
- One rep, one job. Specialization is the unlock.
- Prospecting is engineering, not magic.
