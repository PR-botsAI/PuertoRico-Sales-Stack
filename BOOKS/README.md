# BOOKS

The core literature of selling — organized chronologically and strategically so you can move from **eternal psychology** to **current acquisition frameworks** without losing the thread that connects them.

## Sections

### [01 — Foundational Psychology (1900s — Mid 20th Century)](./01-Foundational-Psychology/)
The books that defined how humans persuade, decide, and buy. If you only read one section, read this one — every modern framework is a reapplication of these principles.

- *How to Win Friends and Influence People* — Dale Carnegie
- *How I Raised Myself from Failure to Success in Selling* — Frank Bettger
- *How to Master the Art of Selling* — Tom Hopkins

- *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* — Robert Cialdini
- *SPIN Selling* — Neil Rackham
- *The One Minute Manager* — Ken Blanchard & Spencer Johnson

## Reading Path (Recommended)

For someone starting from zero in Puerto Rico's sales industry:

1. *How to Win Friends and Influence People* (Carnegie) — relational foundation
2. *How I Raised Myself from Failure to Success in Selling* (Bettger) — discipline and self-management
3. *Influence* (Cialdini) — the six universal levers
4. *SPIN Selling* (Rackham) — questioning framework
5. *Never Split the Difference* (Voss) — negotiation
6. *\$100M Offers* (Hormozi) — modern offer construction
7. *Fanatical Prospecting* (Blount) — pipeline discipline
8. *Made to Stick* (Heath brothers) — communication that lands

That sequence covers roughly 90% of what a top closer needs to internalize before specialization.
