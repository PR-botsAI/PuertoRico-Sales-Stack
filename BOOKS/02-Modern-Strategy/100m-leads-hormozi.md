# $100M Leads — Alex Hormozi (2023)

## Bibliographic

- **Author:** Alex Hormozi
- **First published:** 2023, self-published
- **Era:** Modern Strategy — the prospecting companion to *$100M Offers*
- **Length:** ~330 pages

## Core Thesis

Generating qualified leads is not a marketing problem. It is a four-quadrant operational problem. Every sustainable lead generation system is some combination of four core activities, and you can analyze any business's lead pipeline by mapping its activity to the quadrant.

This book is the operational sequel to *$100M Offers*. Offers tell you *what to sell*. Leads tells you *how to consistently put it in front of the right people*.

## Key Frameworks

### The Core Four — Lead Generation Quadrants

Every method of generating leads falls into one of four boxes, defined by two axes: warm vs cold, one-to-one vs one-to-many.

1. **Warm Outreach** (one-to-one, warm audience) — reaching out to people who already know you. Hormozi argues every operator must do this *first* and *daily* until the warm pool is exhausted. Most operators skip it because it feels low-status.
2. **Post Free Content** (one-to-many, warm audience) — social media, email newsletter, podcast, YouTube. Builds asset value; long compounding curve.
3. **Cold Outreach** (one-to-one, cold audience) — cold email, cold DM, cold call. Highest control, hardest mental work.
4. **Run Paid Ads** (one-to-many, cold audience) — capital-for-speed conversion.

A mature lead engine runs all four simultaneously. Operators who run one or two have ceiling problems they will hit and not understand.

### Lead Magnets — The Five Rules

A lead magnet is a free thing offered in exchange for contact information. Hormozi's rules:

1. **Reveals a problem** the prospect did not fully realize they had
2. **Provides a partial solution** (enough to be valuable, not enough to remove need for the paid offer)
3. **Showcases your competence** in a way that pre-positions the paid offer
4. **Is easy to consume** (10 minutes or less of effort)
5. **Has a high perceived value** relative to its actual cost to produce

The great lead magnet is not a generic PDF. It is a focused solve to a specific painful problem that, once felt, sets up the larger paid solve.

### The Hook — Cold Outreach Mechanics

For cold outreach, the hook is everything. Hormozi's structure:

1. **Call out the specific person** (specific role, specific situation)
2. **Reference their specific problem** (in their language)
3. **Offer a specific outcome** (with specific timeframe)
4. **Provide an easy yes** (low-commitment next step)

No selling in the first message. The message's job is to earn the second message. The second message's job is to earn the call. The call's job is the sale.

### The Engagement Compounding Loop

For posted free content, the loop:

- Post → Engage early viewers → Earn algorithmic boost → Reach broader audience → Capture interested few → Convert to email/DM → Convert to call

The trap most creators fall into: optimizing for views instead of for the conversion at each loop stage. Views are vanity. The actual question is what percentage of viewers became leads, and what percentage of leads became customers.

### Affiliates, Partners, and Employees

Hormozi's three lever-multipliers for the Core Four. Once you have a working method, you scale it by adding people who also do it on your behalf:

- **Affiliates** (paid per result) — lowest commitment, lowest control
- **Partners** (mutual value exchange) — higher trust, higher complexity
- **Employees** (full commitment, full control) — most capital-intensive but most leveraged

## Principles & Tactics

**Generate leads daily, not in bursts.** The single largest behavioral mistake operators make is generating leads in bursts when they need money and stopping when pipeline is full. The discipline is daily activity in all four quadrants, sustained regardless of current need.

**Volume before optimization.** Hormozi's argument: before you optimize a cold email script, send a thousand of the unoptimized version. Optimization on small volume is statistical noise. Volume reveals the real problems.

**Lead magnets, not bait.** A lead magnet that delivers genuine value sets up the paid offer. A lead magnet that is bait-and-switch destroys trust and produces leads who do not buy. The asymmetry is steep.

**Niching the message.** The same lead engine, with the same offer, with the message niched to a specific sub-segment, produces dramatically higher response than the generic version. "Helps businesses grow" reaches no one. "Helps dental practices in PR add 20 new patients monthly" reaches dental practices in PR.

**Track the math.** Cost per lead, cost per acquisition, lifetime value, payback period. Operators who do not track these numbers are running the engine blind and cannot tell which input to scale.

## Notable Concepts

**The lead vs the lead-flow.** Most operators think they need more leads. They actually need a sustainable lead-flow — a system that produces leads predictably. The difference is the difference between begging and operating.

**The unfair-advantage stack.** When you find a quadrant that works, you stack advantages within it: better hooks, better lead magnets, faster follow-up, more touches per lead. Each stack-layer compounds.

**The leaderboard test.** Hormozi argues if you cannot tell which of your channels is winning this week, you are not running a lead system — you are running a guess. Score them weekly.

## Critique & Context

Hormozi's lead-gen frameworks are heavily calibrated for digital-native businesses — information products, online services, scalable B2B SaaS, agencies. They transplant to brick-and-mortar with adaptation, but the literal scripts and platform recommendations are oriented toward digital channels.

For purely local, walk-in, foot-traffic businesses, the Core Four still applies but the *Post Free Content* and *Paid Ads* quadrants need substantial localization. "Run Facebook ads to your zip code" works. "Build a global YouTube channel" usually doesn't make sense for a single-location restaurant.

The second critique: Hormozi's lead magnets work best for prospects who are accustomed to information-product funnels. Older prospects, government buyers, and traditional B2B prospects sometimes find lead-magnet funnels off-putting. Adapt the form to the audience.

## Puerto Rico Application

**Warm outreach in PR.** PR commercial networks are dense. The warm-outreach quadrant is more powerful here than in larger, more anonymous markets. Most PR operators have a warm-pool of 200–500 commercial contacts that has never been systematically worked. Hormozi's argument is to work it first, fully, before any cold activity.

**Bilingual content.** Posted free content in PR should typically be bilingual or sequential — the same idea published in Spanish and in English, separately optimized for each language's platform conventions. Cross-translation rarely produces native-feeling content in either.

**Paid ads, locally targeted.** Meta and Google ad targeting at municipal granularity in PR is achievable and often surprisingly inexpensive. Hyperlocal paid ad campaigns (San Juan, Bayamón, Caguas) outperform island-wide generic targeting in most B2B categories.

**Affiliates and partners in a small market.** The Core Four's multipliers are particularly powerful in PR because the market is small enough that a single well-positioned affiliate or partner can move meaningful volume. Conversely, a bad partner can do brand damage quickly.

## Reading Priority

**Required immediately after *$100M Offers*.** Reading order matters here: offer first, then leads. Reading them in reverse produces a lead engine pointing at a weak offer.

**Reading time:** ~8 hours plus implementation.

**Re-read cadence:** Quarterly review of the Core Four against your actual activity. Almost every operator is over-invested in one or two quadrants and underweight in the others.
