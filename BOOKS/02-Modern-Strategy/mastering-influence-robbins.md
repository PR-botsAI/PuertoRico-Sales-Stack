# Mastering Influence
*by Tony Robbins · originally a 10-CD audio program, 1990s, continuously updated*

## Era & Classification
Late-1990s through present. A program more than a book, but distilled into widely-circulated workbooks and transcripts. The bridge between **classic Carnegie/Ziglar relational psychology** and **modern state-management closing**.

## Overview
Robbins built *Mastering Influence* as a 10-session immersive deep-dive into why people buy. Unlike technique-stack books, this one starts with the seller's own psychology — the premise being that you cannot influence others past the ceiling of your own conviction, certainty, and state.

The program then layers on outward-facing skills: rapport, language patterns, values elicitation, objection neutralization, and closing as a service rather than a forced ending. Robbins synthesizes Bandler/Grinder NLP, Cialdini's influence levers, Carnegie's relational frame, and his own *Awaken the Giant Within* / *Unlimited Power* state-management work.

## Core Premise
**Influence is the byproduct of certainty plus contribution.** Sellers who are uncertain — about the product, the price, or their own worth — telegraph it through micro-signals the buyer reads instantly. Sellers who genuinely believe they're helping (contribution frame) become magnetic. Everything else is mechanics on top of these two ingredients.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The Six Human Needs
Robbins maps every buying decision back to six core needs: certainty, variety, significance, love/connection, growth, and contribution. Top closers learn to identify which 2-3 needs a given prospect is buying to satisfy, then position the offer as the highest-quality delivery vehicle for those specific needs.

### 2. State Management as a Sales Skill
State — your physiology, focus, and language in the moment — drives outcome more than script. Robbins teaches priming routines (incantations, power moves, breath work) before high-stakes calls. A seller in peak state with an average pitch will outperform a seller in a poor state with a perfect pitch.

### 3. Values Elicitation
Before presenting any solution, elicit the prospect's hierarchy of values around the decision. The question framework: *"What's important to you about [outcome]? And what's important about that? And if you had that, what would that give you?"* This stacks values until you reach the emotional driver, which is what you actually sell to.

### 4. Pattern Interrupts
When a prospect is stuck in a loop — same objection, same hesitation — interrupt the pattern physically or linguistically. Stand up. Change the topic abruptly. Ask a question that breaks the script. Then reframe and re-anchor before the old pattern reasserts itself.

### 5. Reframing Objections
Every objection contains a hidden value. "It's too expensive" usually means "I don't yet see the value I'd need to justify the price to myself or my spouse." Robbins's framework: validate the surface objection, dig for the underlying value, then connect your offer to that value.

### 6. The Quality Question Habit
The quality of your life is determined by the quality of the questions you habitually ask. In sales: the quality of your close rate is determined by the quality of the questions you habitually ask prospects. Lower-tier sellers ask "do you want to buy?" Top closers ask questions that get the prospect to sell themselves.

## Signature Frameworks

### The CANI Sales Loop
*Constant And Never-ending Improvement.* After every call, three questions: What worked? What didn't? What will I do differently next time? Built into a daily journaling habit, this compounds into mastery faster than any course.

### The Decision-Making Hierarchy
Robbins's decision triad: 1) What am I going to focus on? 2) What does it mean? 3) What am I going to do? Apply to your own performance and to coaching your prospect through their buying decision.

### The Conviction Test
Before selling something, ask: *Would I pay this price for this product if I were the prospect?* If hesitation, address your own belief gap first. Sellers who haven't fully bought from themselves struggle to get others to buy.

## Why Top Performers Cite It
Robbins coached financial advisors, real-estate teams, and enterprise sales orgs through their highest-revenue years. The program is referenced by Cardone, Hormozi, and most modern closing coaches as the foundational state-management curriculum. Top performers don't necessarily quote the techniques — they internalize the *state-first* premise.

## Application in the Puerto Rico Industry
PR sellers often default to charm and warmth (relationship culture) but stop short on the value-elicitation and conviction layers. *Mastering Influence* is the antidote:
- **Bilingual values elicitation** — running the values-stack question in Spanish often surfaces deeper emotional drivers than English, because the prospect is thinking in their primary inner language.
- **State priming before island time** — PR's slower pace doesn't excuse low-state selling. A 5-minute priming routine before every appointment fixes the energy gap.
- **Conviction in a price-sensitive market** — PR buyers test sellers harder on price because median household income is lower than the US mainland. Robbins's *conviction test* forces the seller to resolve their own price discomfort before facing the prospect.

## How to Study This Program
1. Listen to one session per week (10 weeks).
2. Journal one CANI loop entry per day after sales calls.
3. Run the values-elicitation question on 5 live prospects per week.
4. Pair with *Never Split the Difference* for the in-conversation tactics layer.
