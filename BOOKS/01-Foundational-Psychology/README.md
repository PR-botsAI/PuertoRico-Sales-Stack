# 01 — Foundational Psychology (1900s — Mid 20th Century)

The books in this section established the psychology that **every** modern framework rebuilds. Carnegie, Bettger, Hopkins, Ziglar, Tracy, Cialdini, Rackham, and Blanchard cover the four pillars that have never changed:

1. **Human nature in interaction** — how people respond to being heard, valued, and respected (Carnegie).
2. **Self-discipline of the seller** — the inner mechanics of consistent performance (Bettger, Hopkins, Ziglar, Tracy).
3. **The universal levers of influence** — the cognitive shortcuts buyers actually use (Cialdini).
4. **Structured questioning** — how to surface need and build pain (Rackham).

Plus a fifth pillar that crosses into management:

5. **One-page leadership** — how to manage selling teams without breaking them (Blanchard).
